1. Definition and Meaning

[cite_start]

Virtual Reality (VR) refers to a computer-generated, interactive, three-dimensional environment that fully immerses users, enabling them to experience and interact with digital simulations as if physically present[cite: 11004]. [cite_start]VR uses head-mounted displays (HMDs), motion tracking, and often spatial audio to create this sense of presence[cite: 11005]. [cite_start]Its main characteristics are believability, interactivity, immersion, and computer generation[cite: 11006].

Key Concepts

    [cite_start]
  • Immersion: Users feel transported into the simulation[cite: 11008].
    • [cite_start]
  • Presence: Psychological sense of ‚Äúbeing‚Äù in the virtual world[cite: 11009].
    • [cite_start]
  • Interactivity: Real-time manipulation and response to user actions[cite: 11010].

11. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) and Expert Tips

Q: What hardware is needed for VR?
[cite_start]A: HMD, hand controllers, motion sensors, capable computer or console[cite: 11091].

Q: How to minimize motion sickness?
[cite_start]A: Maintain ‚â•90 FPS, smooth movement, realistic interactions, allow frequent breaks[cite: 11092, 11174, 11161, 11175].

Q: How to design effective VR interfaces?
[cite_start]A: Focus on 3D UIs, ergonomic spatial placement, multimodal feedback, intuitive navigation, and short exposure periods[cite: 11093, 11175].
