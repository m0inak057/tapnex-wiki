Best home workouts for beginners

Published: Tapnex team | 10 min read

Best Home Workouts for Beginners: A Comprehensive Guide

Are you looking for a convenient and effective way to stay fit from the comfort of your own home? Bodyweight exercises are an excellent option, and with the right routine, you can improve your strength, flexibility, and overall well-being. In this article, we will guide you through the best home workouts for beginners, providing you with a comprehensive guide to get you started on your fitness journey.

Benefits of Bodyweight Exercises

Before we dive into the exercises, let's discuss the benefits of bodyweight workouts. Bodyweight exercises are a great way to: * Build strength and muscle mass without the need for equipment * Improve flexibility and mobility * Enhance core stability and balance * Burn fat and improve overall cardiovascular fitness * Increase flexibility and range of motion

Beginner Routine: 10 Essential Exercises

The following exercises are ideal for beginners, providing a full-body workout that can be completed in 15-20 minutes. Remember to complete 2 sets of 10-15 reps for each exercise, with 30 seconds to 1 minute of rest between each move.

1. Bridge

The bridge is a great exercise to start with, activating your core and posterior chain. Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet flat on the floor, and arms extended by your sides. Pushing through your feet and bracing your core, raise your bottom off the ground until your hips are fully extended, squeezing your glutes at the top. Slowly return to the starting position and repeat.

2. Chair Squat

The chair squat is an excellent exercise to improve strength and function in your muscles, tendons, and bones. Stand in front of a chair with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed slightly out. Hinge at your hips and bend your knees, then lower back and down until your bottom touches the chair. Allow your arms to extend out in front of you. Push up through your heels and return to the starting position.

3. Knee Pushup

The knee pushup is a modified version of the standard pushup, ideal for beginners. Get into a high plank position from your knees. Maintaining a straight line from your head to your knees, bend your elbows to lower yourself down to the ground. Keep your elbows at a 45-degree angle. Push back up to start.

4. Stationary Lunge

The stationary lunge is a great exercise to target your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. Split your stance with your right leg in front. Your right foot should be flat on the ground, and your left foot should be up on its toes. Bend your knees and lunge, stopping when your right thigh is parallel to the ground. Push up through your right foot to return to the starting position.

5. Plank to Downward Dog

This exercise will test your upper body, especially your shoulders. Get into a high plank position, with your hands stacked underneath your shoulders and your feet close together. Keeping your core engaged and your hands and feet stationary, pike your hips up and back into the Downward Dog pose. Hold here for a second, then return to the plank.

6. Straight-Leg Donkey Kick

The donkey kick is a great beginner glute exercise. Get on all fours, with your hands aligned beneath your shoulders and your knees beneath your hips. Keeping your back straight, push your right foot out to the imaginary wall behind you while keeping your leg straight.

7. Bird Dog

The bird dog pose is a full-body exercise that requires balance and stability. Get on all fours, ensuring your hands are directly underneath your shoulders and your knees are underneath your hips. Keeping your neck neutral, simultaneously extend your left arm and right leg, keeping your hips square to the ground.

8. Forearm Plank

The forearm plank is a great exercise to improve overall strength, stability, and athletic performance. Assume a plank position on your forearms. Your body should form a straight line from head to feet. Ensure your lower back and hips don’t sag.

9. Side-Lying Hip Abduction

This exercise will target your hip muscles, often neglected in our daily lives. Lie on your left side, supporting your head with the left hand and placing the right hand in front of your body. Keeping your legs straight, stack the right leg over the left leg, keeping your hips pointed forward.

10. Bicycle Crunch

The bicycle crunch is an excellent exercise to target your rectus abdominis muscle, also known as the ‘six pack.’ Lie on your back and bring your legs to a tabletop position. Bend your elbows, and put your hands behind your head. Crunch up and bring your right elbow to your left knee, straightening your right leg.

Intermediate Routine: Progress to the Next Level

Once you've mastered the beginner routine, it's time to progress to the intermediate level. The following exercises will challenge your strength, flexibility, and balance. Remember to complete 2 sets of 10-15 reps for each exercise, with 1 minute of rest between each move.

1. Bridged March

This exercise takes the bridge to the next level, targeting your lower body. Assume a bridge position. Lift one foot off the ground while keeping your leg bent, then bring the foot down. Repeat this movement with the other leg.

2. Squat

The squat is a compound exercise that targets your legs, glutes, and core. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed slightly out. Hinge at your hips and bend your knees, lowering your body down until your thighs are parallel to the ground.

3. Pushup

The pushup is a classic exercise that targets your chest, shoulders, and triceps. Assume a high plank position and lower your body down until your chest almost touches the ground.

4. Forward and Backward Lunge

This exercise will target your lower body muscles, improving stability, mobility, and balance. Start with your feet together and step forward, lunging with your right leg. Push off the forward leg to come back through the starting position, and step back into a backward lunge.

5. Pike Pushup

The pike pushup is a variation of the standard pushup, targeting your shoulders and triceps. Assume a pike position and bend at the elbows, allowing them to flare out to the sides.

6. Kneeling Squat

The kneeling squat is an excellent exercise to target your legs and glutes. Drop down into a squat position, keeping your back straight and your knees behind your toes.

7. Superman

The superman is an excellent exercise to target your lower back and glutes. Lie on your stomach, arms and legs extended. Keeping your neck neutral, recruit your core and the back of your body to simultaneously raise your arms and legs up and off the ground as high as they’ll go.

8. Plank with Alternating Leg Lift

This exercise will challenge your core strength and balance. Assume a plank position and lift one leg up, holding it for 5 seconds, and then return it to the ground. Repeat with the other leg.

9. Kneeling Side Plank with Hip Abduction

This exercise will target your hip muscles and improve your core strength. Kneel on your left side, supporting your body with your elbow. Bend knees at 90 degrees with hips facing front, in line with knees.

Conclusion

Bodyweight exercises are a great way to improve your strength, flexibility, and overall fitness from the comfort of your own home. Remember to start with beginner exercises and progress to more challenging moves as you become stronger and more confident. Always prioritize proper form and technique to avoid injury and reap the benefits of bodyweight workouts.
